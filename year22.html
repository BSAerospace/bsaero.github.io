2022-2023

President and Co-Founder

Erin Levesque

She/They

Erin graduated cum laude in April 2022 with a BSE in Aerospace Engineering. Erin created BSA in February 2020, and she has worked tirelessly ever since to build community among Black Aerospace students at UMich. She is also a member of Michigan Aeronautical Science Association (MASA), where she served as Recovery Project lead, and as Aerodynamics and Recovery Technical lead. Erin is a fellow in the Matthew Isakowitz Fellowship Program, which connects exceptional current college juniors, seniors, and graduate students with paid summer internships in the exciting field of commercial spaceflight, as well as with notable aerospace leaders for mentorship. During Summer 2022, Erin will intern in California for ABL Space Systems, where she will gain practical aerospace engineering work experience. She plans to come back to UMich in Fall 2022 to pursue a master’s degree in aerodynamics and propulsion. In her free time, Erin loves to travel, hike, watch films, and cook. She is from the Chicago area, and has a pet cat named Apollo.

DEI Representative and Co-Founder

Prince Kuevor

He/Him

Although he is not an actual prince, Prince has served BSA very well as a co-founder, and as our first Aerospace Department Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI) Committee Liaison. He completed his bachelor's and master's degrees in Aerospace Engineering here at the University of Michigan, focusing on flight dynamics and controls. Through his work in the Michigan eXploration Lab (MXL), Prince has worked on two CubeSats that have been launched into Low Earth Orbit. Prince is now a PhD candidate of the Robotics Department, where he has worked on projects that use groups of drones to carry heavy objects and detect wildfires in the western United States. His dissertation work focuses on using locally-generated magnetic field maps to improve navigation capabilities in GPS-denied environments (e.g. inside buildings). With this work, Prince hopes to enable better indoor navigation for low-cost applications. Prince is expected to defend his dissertation in the Fall of 2022 before working in industry as a perception and controls engineer. In his free time, Prince likes to play volleyball and board games.

Co-Treasurer

Eric Hersey

He/Him

Eric graduated magna cum laude in April 2022 with a BSE in Aerospace Engineering. He served as BSA's first Treasurer, and will continue to do so this upcoming year as he lends his expertise to our incoming Co-Treasurers. He is also involved in Minorities in Agriculture, Natural Resources, and Related Sciences (MANRRS), as well as with the National Society of Black Engineers (NSBE). In his time at UMich, Eric has interned as a Software Engineer for Rockwell Automation, and was a two-time Aerospace Engineering Intern for GE Aviation. He has also worked with an on-campus research group to study spherical bubble dynamics in non-Newtonian biological tissues. During Summer 2022, Eric will be a Quality Systems Engineer Intern with Lockheed Martin. He will return to UMich in Fall 2022 to further his education with a master’s degree in Aerospace Engineering. Outside of class, Eric enjoys culinary artistry and spending time with his friends. He recently made a beef stew with only ingredients from the school’s free pantry. Blending free goods with exciting spices, he self-admittedly is blazing a new path for students interested in cooking while maintaining a livable budget amidst student loans, rent, and book fees. Eric is currently training to compete in a cook-off with professional staff at UMich’s Campus Farm and continues to push his limits as a chef. He has competed in two student-organized cooking competitions and is currently sitting at 0-2 in his professional record. With each loss, he cultivates his craft and improves upon past failures. One day, he hopes to be a top chef and eventually owner of his own esteemed restaurant in one of the top food cities in America, such as Las Vegas or Charleston, South Carolina.

DEI Representative and Co-Events Coordinator

Elijah Simpson

He/Him

Elijah is a freshman in Aerospace Engineering, and our current Co-Events Coordinator and DEI Representative. He is also a recipient of the Patti Grace Smith Fellowship. This program is specifically focused on Black students currently enrolled in the first or second year of a higher education program who are seeking their first work experience in the aerospace industry. Students are selected through a rigorous review process that includes aerospace industry professionals, rising-star early career employees, and corporate employers, particularly seeking scholars with an exceptional aptitude for their discipline as well as a display of creativity, ingenuity, and a commitment to serving others. Elijah will spend Summer 2022 as a Technical Engineering Intern at Northrop Grumman in Utah, where he is “planning to be a sponge. I want to learn how to be an integral member of the team. I plan to listen, ask questions, and form networks with new people.” Elijah’s dream job is to build rockets. His all-time favorite movie is Transformers, and he lived in three different countries as a military kid. Pretty cool!
